# CYA-2-Choose-Your-Own-Adventure-02
This is my submission for CYA-2 for 427

Reflection: Application to ACE Program (Griffiss Institute / AFRL)


Activity Description
The expectation for this activity was to submit a competitive application for the ACE (Advanced Course in Engineering) summer internship program administered by the Griffiss Institute. ACE forges a cadre of cyber warriors and leaders of consequence through an intense, immersive program at the Griffiss Institute and nearby Air Force Research Laboratory in Rome, NY. The program includes graduate-level cyber seminars, open-ended challenge problems with ~20-page solution papers, real-time simulated kinetic and cyber mission operations, leadership case studies, perspectives talks from senior leaders, and a full-summer R&D project whose deliverables will see actual use within the DoD. I applied specifically for the ACE opportunity, which emphasizes technical excellence in contested cyber environments, servant leadership, and high-tempo operations (80–100 hours/week). I researched the program requirements (including eligibility for security clearance, strong STEM academic background, and alignment with the ACE vision and 4 C’s core values), thoroughly reviewed the official ACE Interview Preparation Guide, tailored my resume and supporting materials to highlight relevant coursework and skills, and completed the formal application process through the designated channels. This included preparing for the 1-hour virtual interview with Mr. Eric Royer. My experience with the MAVEN internship program has also helped me familiarize myself with the interview process and what to expect.


Technical or Professional Decisions
Key decisions included strategically aligning all application materials with ACE’s vision, “to forge a cadre of cyber warriors and leaders of consequence” and the 4 C’s core values which are made up of Competence, Commitment, Courage, and Compassion. I chose to highlight transferable skills and demonstrated interest in national security applications—particularly cybersecurity, networking, signal processing, software development, and problem-solving in uncertain/contested environments—rather than generic academic achievements. I explicitly prepared examples tied to each core value and reflected on a substantive life failure (beyond a simple academic setback) as requested in the interview expectations. Professionally, I decided to pursue this opportunity independently to gain direct exposure to DoD operational environments, clearance processes, and the unique intensity of the ACE program. I prioritized clarity, specificity, and professionalism in my submission to stand out among applicants for this highly competitive, high-workload position. I also made sure to highlight the certificate I received which was Security+. I heard about this certificate from the MAVEN internship I participated in last summer.


Contributions
This was an individual application process with no team involvement. I independently managed all aspects: researching the ACE program and its logistical details (travel stipend, dormitory housing in Rome, NY, expected 8-mile run standards, etc.), reviewing the full interview preparation guide, updating and tailoring my resume and supporting documents, preparing responses for the technical/non-technical interview questions, and submitting a complete application on time.


Quality Assessment
I assess my performance as strong in preparation and execution. The application was thorough, well-targeted to the program’s vision and values, and submitted professionally. I closely internalized the ACE core values and logistical expectations and prepared concrete examples for the required discussion of one core value and a meaningful failure. If repeating the experience, I would improve by seeking feedback on my materials from mentors or prior ACE participants earlier and by strengthening any gaps in demonstrated cyber-specific projects or leadership examples. Overall, the process reinforced disciplined self-presentation under tight deadlines and gave me a clear understanding of the program’s demanding yet supportive nature.


Career Alignment
This application directly advances my career goals in defense technology and national security. Gaining acceptance into the ACE program would provide hands-on experience in operational cyber missions, graduate-level technical challenges, real-world DoD R&D with immediate mission impact, leadership development through case studies and senior-leader perspectives, and immersion in a program explicitly designed to produce cyber warriors and leaders of consequence. It would offer exposure to Air Force Research Laboratory culture, potential security clearance sponsorship, and a direct pathway toward full-time roles in cyber operations. Even the application and interview preparation process itself built critical skills in professional networking with mission-focused organizations and helped clarify my interest in transitioning academic STEM knowledge into impactful defense contributions. I am also an SFS recipient, therefore this internship satisfies the requirements needed for the scholarship.
